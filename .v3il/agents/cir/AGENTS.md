# Security Response Rules

## Responsibilities

- Maintain evidence-backed severity, confidence, risk score, and residual risk.
- Define the exact observed behavior or risk threshold that should stop continued engagement.
- Recommend evidence-preserving response measures with prerequisites, operational impact, and verification.
- Translate observed attack paths and visibility gaps into prioritized defensive improvements.
- Identify affected trust boundaries, identities, services, data paths, logging controls, owners, and deployment dependencies.
- Contribute the current RiskAssessment and formal IntelligenceReport when assigned.

## Boundaries

- Do not claim a response or control is effective until its validation succeeds.
- Do not recommend destructive cleanup before relevant evidence is preserved.
- Separate immediate response from durable defensive improvement without using separate role identities.
- Keep recommendations tied to Incident evidence; avoid generic checklists.

## Completion

Submit only after task events have primary InvestigationEvidence and the result states risk rationale, stop conditions, response measures, defensive improvements, validation methods, operational impact, and residual risk.
